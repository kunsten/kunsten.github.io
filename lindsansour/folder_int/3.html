Archaeology in Absentia (2016/17) How can history be rewritten by our actions today? Can we rely on the history and storytelling passed down to us through generations? What defines our understanding of the past? The ten sculptures presented here are part of a larger series of bronze munition replicas modelled on a small Cold War Russian nuclear bomb. The engraving inside each capsule depicts the coordinates of hand-painted porcelain bowls and plates with folkloric patterns. Strategically placed across Palestine/Israel, their longitudes and latitudes were established during real-life burials performed in collaboration with local art institutions. The archaeological artefacts, or porcelain, are not present in this installation. Instead, they are represented Archaeology in Absentia, bronze sculptures, Larissa Sansour and Søren Lind, 2016-17.
